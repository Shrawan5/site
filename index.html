About Me

I work at the intersection of storytelling, media, and social change. Trained in Journalism and Mass Communication, I have spent the past several years using communication as a tool—not just to inform, but to connect, question, and inspire action. My professional journey spans the development sector, where I have worked with NGOs and community-based organizations as a Program and Communication Officer.

Photography has been a core part of my creative practice since 2020. Through images, I document people, places, and moments that often go unnoticed. For me, photography is not decoration—it is evidence, memory, and emotion.

I am deeply interested in how digital media and artificial intelligence are reshaping communication, particularly in Nepal and South Asia. My work is guided by a belief that meaningful stories, when told responsibly, can create understanding—and understanding can lead to change.
