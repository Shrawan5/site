About Me

I work at the intersection of storytelling, media, and social change. Trained in Journalism and Mass Communication, I have spent the past several years using communication as a tool—not just to inform, but to connect, question, and inspire action. My professional journey has taken me into the development sector, where I have worked as a Program Officer and Communication Officer with NGOs and community-based organizations, shaping narratives around education, youth engagement, and social awareness.

Beyond written words, I speak through images. Photography has been a core part of my creative practice since 2020, allowing me to document people, places, and moments that often go unnoticed. For me, a photograph is not just visual content—it is evidence, memory, and emotion.

I am deeply interested in how technology is reshaping communication—from digital media to artificial intelligence— particularly in the context of Nepal and South Asia. Curious by nature and grounded in purpose, I believe that meaningful stories, when told responsibly, have the power to create understanding—and understanding can lead to change.
